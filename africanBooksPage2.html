    So Long a Letter by Mariama Ba

    So Long a letter is a short novel, originally written in French by Mariama Ba and later translated to English. The novel is one of the most profound texts that introduces the contradiction existing in terms of religion, modernity and tradition.

    A Moonless, Starless Sky by Alexis Okeowo

    Alexis Okeowo in her non-fiction book, A Moonless, Starless Sky, took us on a journey of the realities of four African countries. She attempted in such beautiful fashion, to rid Africa of the single narrative of underdevelopment, oppression, religious extremism and terrorism, and showed how ordinary people in their unique way fight against these.....

    The secret Lives of Baba Segi's Wives by Lola Shoneyin

    Lola Shoneyin's debut novel is one that exposes the realities of a polygamous home. The amalgam of power, weakness, secrecy and hypocrisy was fully displayed as you pore through the pages. You have a full blown ignorant patriarch called Baba Segi who took pride in his virility and made it a duty to satisfy his wives in the bid to prove his masculinity.

    Be(com)ing Nigerian by ElNathan John

    The 145-page satire chronicles every facet of decadence in the Nigerian system – top to bottom - from politics, religion, governance, tribalism, non-governmental organizations, migration, the artistic burden of being a writer and many more. Whilst the author may have adequately discussed a lot of issues, the theme of religion remains constant.     Unbowed by Wangari Maathai

    The grace and poise in which she beautifully accepted her Nobel Peace Prize in 2004, had every Kenyan want to hug a tree out of love and admiration. Even with her new found world fame, her vision was clear and precise, to continue planting trees. Contrary to her advisors who wanted her to tour the world and launch campaigns that would attract donor funds. She chose home, where her heart was. Requests from local primary schools to plant trees, she gave the same weight and attention, as invitations to speak at Oxford....

    We are the ones we need is a descriptive and heartbreaking biography by Sihle Bolani. Sihle shares her experience
