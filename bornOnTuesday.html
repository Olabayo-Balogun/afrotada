    Born On Tuesday

    by ElNathan John

    • 16 February 2021
    • Adebayo Olatundee

    Born on a Tuesday, the debut novel of the two-time Caine Prize finalist Elnathan John is one that tells the story of Dantala's journey as a young man who had to study under a sheik only to find himself associated with kids devoid of morals and pawns in the hands of political juggernauts.

    Dantala after surviving the chaos that occured due to the election result resorted to going back to his parent's home as he could no longer stay under the Kuka tree. On his way, he met a sheik who became an important figure in his life who provided shelter and guidance for him. It wasn't long before calamity erupted as one of the shiek's confidant began to raise a radical movement of his own.

    This story is one that explains how Dantala navigated all hese obstacles, how he survived living under the Kuka tree, the political tension in northern Nigeria and the religious fundamentalism and violence he faced each day. It is a book that would leave you in tears because this isn't just fiction, it is the experience of young people who are victim of a society they were born in. I have to warn you, if you are looking for a happy ending, I suggest you read a fairy tale. This book isn't a " happily ever after" tale, it's about the realities of boys under the Kuka tree who have no other option to feed themselves than being used as political tools, it's about the realities of losing your loved one due to governmental negligence. It's about the realities of people with no power, money or name.
