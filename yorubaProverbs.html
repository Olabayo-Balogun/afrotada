    • AFRICAN PROVERBS

    Yoruba - Nigeria

    Proverb

    Adie funfun ko mo ara relagba

    Meaning

    Respect yourself

    Proverb

    ile la ti n ko eso ro de

    Meaning

    Charity begins at home

    Proverb

    Díẹ díẹ nimú ẹlẹ dẹ ẹ fi ń wọgbà

    Meaning

    Little by little is how the pig’s nose enters the yard

    Proverb

    À ń pe gbẹ nàgbẹ nà ẹyẹ àkókó ń yọjú

    Meaning

    A sculptor is summoned and the woodpecker shows up

    Proverb

    Ọbẹ kìí gbé inú àgbà mì

    Meaning

    The soup does not move round in an elder’s belly

    Proverb

    Eewu bę loko Longę, Longę fun ara rę eewu ni

    Meaning

    here is danger at Longę's farm (Longę is a name of a Yoruba Legend), Longę himself is danger.

    Proverb

    Bi Ēēgun nla ba ni ohùn o ri gontò, gontò na a ni ohùn o ri Ēēgun nla

    Meaning

    If a big masquerade claims it doesn't see the smaller masquerade, the small masquerade will also claim it doesn't see the big masquerade.

    Proverb

    Kò sí ęni tí ó ma gùn ęşin tí kò ní ju ìpàkó. Bí kò fę ju ìpàkó, ęşin tí ó ngùn á ję kojū

    Meaning

    No one rides a horse without moving his head, voluntarily or involuntarily.

    Proverb

    Bí abá so òkò sójà ará ilé eni ní bá

    Meaning

    He who throws a stone in the market will hit his relative. Meaning: avoid doing things that will have serious repercussions or effects

    Proverb

    A pę ko to jęun, ki ję ibaję .

    Meaning

    The person that eats late, will not eat spoiled food. Meaning: It is more profitable to exercise patience while seeking a durable solution, in difficult situations than to hastily accept an ill- conceived/prepared solution
