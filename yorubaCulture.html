    Yoruba culture

    • 01 June 2021
    • Serafina Kithinji

    The Yoruba of people of West Africa who live chiefly in southwest Nigeria came into existence more than one thousand years ago. Accounts say they originate from Mecca in Saudi Arabia though Oduduwa. The Yorubas can be found in Oyo, Ogun, Osun, Ondo, Ekiti and Dkwara as well as Lagos states, parts of Kogi state of Nigeria and the adjoining parts of Benin republic and Togo, commonly known as Yorubaland. Oduduwa landed at Ife life and taught people the Ede Yoruba, Oduduwa is the father of all Yorubas.

    The Yoruba people believe that all human beings pass through what is known as Ayanmo which translates to destiny or fate. Furthermore the thoughts and actions of each person in Aye (the physical realm) interact with all living things including the earth itself. Each person living on earth attempts to achieve perfection and find their destiny in Orun-ree (the spiritual realm of those who do good and beneficial things) ones Ori-inu (spiritual consciousness in the physical realm) must grow in order to consummate union with ones Iponri (Ori orun spiritual self) Iwapele (or well balanced) meditative recitation and sincere veneration is sufficient to strengthen the Ori-inu of most people. Well-balanced people, it is believed they are able to make positive use of the simplest form of connection between their Oris and the omnipotent Olu-orun: an adura (petition or prayer) for divine support. Prayer to one's Ori Orun produces an immediate sensation of joy. Eshu Elegbara initiates contact with the spiritual realm on behalf of the petitioner, and transmits the prayer to Aye; the deliverer of Ase or the spark of life. He transmits this prayer without distorting it in any way. Thereafter, the petitioner may be satisfied with a personal answer. In the event that he or she is not, the Ifa oracle of the Orisha Orunmila may also be consulted. All communication with orun whether simplistic in the form of a personal prayer or complicated in the form of that done by and initiated by Babalawo (priest of divination), however, is energized by invoking Ase.

    Yoruba religion consists of traditional religious and spiritual concepts and practices. Their god is called Olodumare who is the supreme deity. He is also known as Orise (the source of things), Olorun or Oluwa (lord). He lives in the sky and he is a distant god, and when it comes to everyday prayers, things are often handled by protectors called Orishas. Orishas are spirits/unique/special/deities/divinities/gods selected heads sent by Olodumare for the guidance of all creation and of humanity in particular, on how to live and be successful on earth. They are the intermediaries between Olodumare and the mankind. Orishas have control over specific elements of nature.They are thus also referred to as Imole. There are Orishas that are more supreme than others and are known as deities. Examples of Orishas are Orunmila who is the grand priest and custodian of Ifa oracle, source of knowledge who cures illnesses and deformities: Eshu; who deals with a hand of misfortunes to those that do not offer tribute or are deemed to be spiritual novices. He is the negotiator between positive and negative forces: Ogun is the lord of iron and metallurgy: Yemoja is the mother of waters and is believed to be amniotic fluid in the womb of a pregnant woman as well as breasts that nurture. She is considered the protective energy of the feminine force. Osun is associated with several powers including abilities to heal with cool water, induction of fertility and the control of the feminine essence. She elevates women disorders and she is fond of babies, she is sought if a baby becomes ill and she is known for her love of honey. Among others are Sango associated with virility and masculinity and also fire lighting, and Oya who is the guardian of the cemetery, winds of change, storms and progression. Yoruba also believes in Irunmole who are entities sent by Olorun to complete given tasks, Often acting as liaisons between Orun(the invisible realm) and Aiye (the physical realm). Irunmole can best be described as ranking divinities; whereby such divinities are regarded as the principal Orishas. She is the Orisha of rebirth. Oludumare rules over the universe and he has no gender.

    The Yorubas are embodied in Ifa divination and are known as the tripartite book of enlightenment in Yorubaland and in its diaspora. Olodumare gave Emi (the breath of life) to humankind. In this, Olodumare is supreme, and is considered the principal agent of creation. According to one of Yoruba beliefs of creation, at a certain stage in the process, the truth was sent to confirm the habitability of the planets that were newly formed. The earth being one of these was visited but considered too wet for conventional living. After sometime a number of divinities led by Obatala were sent to accomplish the mission of helping the earth develop its crust which he did gradually to a point where he made a large patch of dry land; the various indentations they made became hills and valleys. Obatala then named the place Ife. The land became fertile and plants began to grow and flourish. Olodumare also created explosions and began to bake and then released the breath of life to blow across the land and figurines slowly came into being as the first people of Ife. For this reason, Ife is locally referred to as Ife Oodaye –cradle of existence.

    The Yorubas believe in Atunwa, reincarnation within the family. The names Babatunde (father returns, Yetunde (mother returns) , Babatunji (father wakes once again) and Sotunde (the wise man returns) all offer vivid evidence of the Ifa concept of family or lineal rebirth. There is no simple guarantee that your grandfather or great uncle will come back in the birth of your child, however whenever the time arrives for a spirit to return to earth through the conception of a new life in the direct bloodline of the family one of the component entities of a person’s being returns, while the other remains in heaven (Ikole Orun).The spirit that returns does so in the form of a guardian Ori.
