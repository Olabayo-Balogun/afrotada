    South Africa's culture

    • 02 June 2021
    • Charles Karanja Munderu

    South Africa is popularly known as the Rainbow Nation. This is a title that describes the country's ethnic and cultural diversity. South Africa is among the multi-cultural countries on the globe. Different ethnic groups living in urban areas are part of the population. South Africa comprises of black people (dominant) while immigration during the slave trade era and colonization brought the Chinese, Indians, white Europeans, amongst others into the country.

    Languages

    South Africa is home to 11 formal languages. However, the main languages that are commonly used are IsiZulu and isiXhosa. English is also commonly spoken because the multi-ethnic composition of the country. It is fascinating to know that South Africa’s official languages in the past were actually European (Afrikaans, English). That has drastically changed over the years, especially after the end of apartheid in 1994. Historically, South Africa has 34 recognized languages, thirty are the existing languages, while four are non-existent Khoe-San languages.

    Food

    It is important to note that South Africa boasts of a great cultural mix, due to this, the country has a wide variety of different food choices. This is from the traditional food to cosmopolitan cuisine from other countries. Vegetables and maize (corn) are the primary staple foods for South Africa. The Asian population in the country particularly Indians have their own cuisine which adds variety to the country’s food.

    Music

    South African music is one of the best kind ever. Not only is it prominent in South Africa but also in different parts of the world. The South African musical styles include jazz, traditional indigenous music, and Christian religious music. Incredible music performers have also been a part of the diverse culture. You will learn of the likes of Hugh Masekela, Miriam Makeba, Ladysmith Black Mambazo, Soweto Gospel Choir, Brenda Fassie and many others.

    Art

    Dancing and textile weaving are parts of the traditional art of South Africa. They both describe the country’s ethnic identity that has been long preserved. Modern art forms have also emerged, such as literature, painting etc. There are various public and private initiatives meant to support the culture of the country.

    Sports

    South Africans have a deep passion for sports. Soccer tops as the number one sport in the country. Rugby and cricket follow closely after soccer. Without a doubt, South Africans have shown the world their sporting talents. You cannot separate sports and South Africa. They are basically intertwined. Sports bring together people from all areas. If you live in town or rural areas, sports play a significant role in getting all of us together. This is what happens in South Africa. Wade van Niekerk broke a world record for the 400m race-winning gold. Caster Semenya, on the other hand, won gold in the 800m race. All athletes in South Africa enjoy the support of both the citizens and the goverment

    Fashion

    South African fashion is on another level. It is unique and describes the culture of the country. Fashion designers are always outdoing each other to bring out the best of their talent. The traditional fabrics and prints used are unmatched. There are influential designers in South Africa whose purpose is to showcase the country’s style to the entire world like Thebe Magugu, Rich Mnisi etc.

    Love and gender

    South Africa is not only a beautiful country but also filled with beautiful people. In a world where love and gender have become everyday topics, South Africa has set itself apart. It was the first Africa country to legalize love and marriage between the same sex. It ranks 5th in the world for having this law. The South African constitution recognizes the right of same-sex couple and gender-non conforming individuals.

    South Africa facts and statistics

    • South Africa population: 60,041,994 million as of 2021
    • Land area: 1.21 million km²
    • Capitals: Pretoria, Bloemfontein, Cape Town
    • Religion: Christian (86%), Traditional African (6%), Non-affiliated (5.2%), Muslim (1.9%), Hindu (0.9%)
    • Ethnic groups: African (80.7%), Colored (8.8%), White (8.1%), Asian (2.5%)
    • Languages: English, Ndebele, Northern Sotho, Sotho, Afrikaans, Tswana, Zulu, Xhosa, Tsonga, Venda, Swazi
    • South Africa dialing code: +27
    • Significant industries: Mining, Manufacturing, Tourism
