    Born on Tuesday by ElNathan John

    Born on a Tuesday, the debut novel of the two-time Caine Prize finalist Elnathan John is one that tells the story of Dantala's journey as a young man who had to study under a sheik only to find himself associated with kids devoid of morals and pawns in the hands of political juggernauts.

    The Army and Politics in Zimbabwe: Mujuru, the Liberation Fighter and Kingmaker by Miles Tendi

    A book with threads of academic rigour to make the concepts the author puts across more valid and believable. The book has multiple strands, the first is a historical narrative of the internal disputes that rocked the liberation movement. It goes into detail to outline the culture that was present in the military movement. Issues such as the neglect of mental health of liberation fighters after the war is highlighted with the author asserting that Solomon Mujuru was a highly functional alcoholic who never got help for his condition which developed during the war.....

    Oil On Water by Helon Habila

    Oil on Water is a short novel by Nigeria’s 2001 Cain Prize winner Helon Habila. It explores the social, moral and environmental ripple effects of oil extraction along the Niger Delta. Based on the recollections of Rufus, a young journalist hunting his biggest story in very dangerous territory, the novel depicts an African atmosphere heavily tainted by human greed, violence, death and moral degradation.

    Stay With Me by Ayobami Adebayo

    Stay With Me is a story of deceit, love, betrayal and pain. It is set in Nigeria’s military regime and tells the story of Yejide, a naïve young virgin who marries the love of her life, Akin in her final year of university. But unknowingly to her, Akin is impotent. Years into the marriage, the pressure of childbirth increased. And like every other typical African society, when children weren’t forthcoming in the marriage, all fingers pointed at Yejide............

    Born A Crime by Trevor Noah

    TThis memoir is culmination of stories that Trevor Noah uses to show the reader his life lesson building blocks. We get an intimate understanding of the socio-political environment of a mischievous young boy that was born a crime in apartheid South Africa. It is written in a series of short essays, each around a specific theme, and not in chronological order. However, this makes it easily digestible.....

    Black Tax: Burden or Ubuntu edited by Niq Mhlongo

    This book is a collection of essays from different authors. It uses the personal experiences of these authors to explore the idea of black tax. The contributions from the different authors investigate the historical roots of black tax and the experiences of black families more specifically the black middle class....
