    So Long A Letter

    by Mariama Ba

    • 22 August 2020
    • Lewis S Waswa

    So Long a letter is a short novel, originally written in French by Mariama Ba and later translated to English. The novel is one of the most profound texts that introduces the contradiction existing in terms of religion, modernity and tradition.

    The author weaves an epic storyline in form of a letter to her friend, in a manner that brings out celebration and defiance of culture; naivety and regret in personal relationships; the complexity of love and the societal indifference to the betrayal and suffering of women. Through Ramatoulaye’s pain and her outpouring to Aissatou, the author walks us through the societal changes, the waning of love and the impacts on the family institution. The reader is pushed to question whether education can truly liberate a woman in the face of all constraining forces. And also, whether it can help a man realize the imbalance of power endowed to him by the society, and in doing so, internalize the inherent faults within.

    What is consistent, however, is the resolve to stick to decency and swallow the pain- a story of millions of women in Africa, then as it is now. As to whether that is a good or bad attribute, a force of nature or nurture, is left for the reader to judge.

    This book epitomizes the pain of women in Africa, and many other places, where both tradition and religion are so infused in the fabric of society, that the oppressed continues to be the tool for further oppression. It is left for the reader to judge whether culture, religion and norms can dynamically move with the times or remain the greatest impediment to the realization of self as far as women are concerned.

    The style of writing, the conveyance of emotions, the wrapping of feelings and the tangible pain presented in the book makes it a repetitive read and a must have if one is to get in touch with the complex attributes of systemic pain.
