    Born A Crime

    by Trevor Noah

    • 16 February 2021
    • Yanga Malotana

    Funny, Intriguing & Loveable

    This memoir is culmination of stories that Trevor Noah uses to show the reader his life lesson building blocks. We get an intimate understanding of the socio-political environment of a mischievous young boy that was born a crime in apartheid South Africa. It is written in a series of short essays, each around a specific theme, and not in chronological order. However, this makes it easily digestible. Trevor Noah manages to cover pertinent issues like colonialism, apartheid, gender roles and education with a comedic demeanour that makes it difficult to put the book down! Born A Crime is the perfect blend of the personal tale of family, friendships and even first crushes. The stories that Trevor Noah shares risk breaking the reader’s heart but that tension is immediately eased with his comedic gold. This memoir was brimming with emotion towards Trevor Noah’s hard-working, strong and powerhouse mother. If you are not an advent reader, the audiobook is just as entertaining and impactful as the book. Trevor Noah is an incredible narrator and manages to make the book more personal and funnier through the audio. This was definitely an enjoyable read and it definitely lived up to its hype!
