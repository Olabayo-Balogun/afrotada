    We are the ones we need

    by Ijangolet Ogwang

    • 22 July 2020
    • Inga Macingwane

    A must read for young black professionals! I was so sure that merit was an actual requirement for the grown-up life that awaited us after graduation. After I started working, disillusionment set in, hard and heavy - Sihle Bolani

    We are the ones we need is a descriptive and heartbreaking biography by Sihle Bolani. Sihle shares her experience of working one of South Africaâ€™s top financial institutions. This biography focuses on structural racism in Corporate SA, the role of senior executives in perpetuating corporate abuse & bullying and the complicity of black executives in structural racism in the workplace.

    Sihle pulls out all the files. The book is written in first person and reads like a diary entry. She shares her interactions with her colleagues & senior executives. She also shares the emotional and psychological impact that she suffered as a result of corporate abuse.

    This book was an emotional roller coaster ride. I admire Sihle for her courage in challenging racism in her workplace.
