    An Image In The Mirror

    by Ijangolet Ogwang

    • 21 September 2020
    • Inga Macingwane

    There it is - the constant expectation hanging over my head. Where we come from, this piece of history that I am not even part of that predefined me; that dictates that I work harder, be better, be smarter. I have lived my life not only for myself, but for the missed opportunities of Aunty and Uncle; for my cousins barred from “a better life” and for the sleeping corpses that house my ancestors’ dreams. - Nyakale⠀

    An image in a mirror is a lovely read about twin sisters separated at birth, a choice made by their Mother who knew she couldn’t take care of both of them so she shared the ‘burden’ with her Sister. They are countries apart but their lives are so in-sync, unbeknownst to them both. They have a lot of questions about the choice their mother made and I guess some underlying resentments towards each other. At the end they find what they’ve both been longing for... Sisterhood.⠀

    The novel is not just a story of a girl in distress but girls all over the world, fighting for their right to education; women and girls clamoring for an end to child marriage and child labor. The novel demonstrates the difference education makes and is capable of through the progress made by Adunni.

    We’re introduced to Nyakale, who is taken by her aunt and raised in South Africa while her twin sister Achen remains in one of the villages in Uganda.It is an eventful journey of how their mother’s decision impacts how they grow up, their experiences inform the decisions they make. What is clear throughout the book is the yearning that a mother and daughters have for each other as well as the strength of the twins’ bond traversing distance.⠀

    The book deals with many societal issues such as xenophobia, colourism, women’s rights, single parenting, mental health, gender-based violence, Rhodes must fall movement, mobility and migration in search for safety or greener pastures, impact of war-torn countries on families, child soldiers, societal expectations, marriage and what it represents, loss and identity.⠀

    I loved everything about this book!
