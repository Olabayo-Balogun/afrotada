    The Bantus Of Kongo

    In central Africa, extending from the Atlantic ocean in the west, to the Kwango river in the east and from the great Congo river in the north, to the Kwanza river in the south reigned one of the greatest and richest cultural civilizations dated back into the stone age up to its sovereignty in the 13th century under their native great king LUKENI LUA NIMI before the Kongo civil war between 1665-1709 and to its abolishment in 1914 by the Portuguese giving rise to secession and the birth of the present day Democratic Republic of Congo....

    Ethiopian culture

    Ethiopia is a land-locked country located in Eastern part of Africa bordering Sudan, South Sudan, Kenya, Somalia, Eritrea, Djibouti. The word Ethiopia means the land of burnt-faced people or the land of black people. The country originated from the ancient Aksumite Kingdom from the first century AD. It was ruled predominantly by the Solomonic Dynasty which ruled the Ethiopian Empire from 1270 to 1975 upon the death of Haile Selassie....

    Yoruba culture

    The Yoruba of people of West Africa who live chiefly in southwest Nigeria came into existence more than one thousand years ago. Accounts say they originate from Mecca in Saudi Arabia though Oduduwa. The Yorubas can be found in Oyo, Ogun, Osun, Ondo, Ekiti and Dkwara as well as Lagos states, parts of Kogi state of Nigeria and the adjoining parts of Benin republic and Togo, commonly known as Yorubaland. Oduduwa landed at Ife life and taught people the Ede Yoruba, Oduduwa is the father of all Yorubas....

    South African culture

    South Africa is popularly known as the Rainbow Nation. This is a title that describes the country's ethnic and cultural diversity. South Africa is among the multi-cultural countries on the globe. Different ethnic groups living in urban areas are part of the population. South Africa comprises of black people (dominant) while immigration during the slave trade era and colonization brought the Chinese, Indians, white Europeans, amongst others into the country.....
