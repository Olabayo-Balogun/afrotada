    A Moonless Starless Sky

    by Alexis Okeowo

    • 18 August 2020
    • Olatunde Adebayo

    Alexis Okeowo in her non-fiction book, A Moonless, Starless Sky, took us on a journey of the realities of four African countries. She attempted in such beautiful fashion, to rid Africa of the single narrative of underdevelopment, oppression, religious extremism and terrorism, and showed how ordinary people in their unique way fight against these maladies that have plagued their countries for decades.

    She started with the bittersweet story of Bosco and Eunice who were captured by the Lord's resistance army in northern Uganda. How they fell in love, escaped the LRA and dealt with being integrated back into a society that was prejudiced against them. She told the heart-wrenching tale of modern day slavery in Mauritania and how activists fought against the marginalisation and slavery of people in Mauritania.

    In the third chapter, she talked about the Boko Haram menace in Northern Nigeria, the shocking escape of a captured young girl and how locals rallied to fight against the insurgency that had ravaged their land. Finally, she wrote about the fight against religious extremism in Somalia and how a couple of young girls continued to play basketball in a society that frowned on women participating in sport.

    A Moonless, Starless Sky is a story of grit, guts and gumption. It is the revelation of the power of ordinary people who wouldn't sit back and let others do their fighting for them. It is not common to see people who would risk their lives to give a true representation of the African reality. Alexis Okeowo gave us a gift and everyone should read it.
