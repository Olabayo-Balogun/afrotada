    "An Image in a Mirror" by Ijangolet Ogwang

    An image in a mirror is a lovely read about twin sisters separated at birth, a choice made by their Mother who knew she couldn’t take care of both of them so she shared the ‘burden’ with her Sister. They are countries apart but their lives are so in-sync, unbeknownst to them both. They have a lot of questions about the choice their mother made and I guess some underlying resentments towards each other. At the end they find what they’ve both been longing for... Sisterhood...

    "A Manifesto For Social Change: How To Save South Africa" by Moeletsi Mbeki

    The book is a light read that is short and easy to digest and analyses the evolution of the social and economic forces from the time of British colonialism, through Afrikaner nationalism and then to the current epoch of African nationalism in South Africa....

    "Solidarity Road" by Jan Theron

    The book talks about the history of the Food Canning Workers Union (FCWU) from 1976 until it was absorbed as an affiliate of the Congress of South African Trade Unions (COSATU), the book is also an autobiographical work of the author’s role in events that led to the restructuring and reformation of the FCWU into a vibrant workers organisation between 1976 until about 1986 when the author left the organisation....

    "Freedom In Our Lifetime" by Anton Lembede

    An interesting book about the writings of the founding president of the African National Congress (ANC) youth league, AM Lembede who held an MA Philosophy and an LLB. Despite coming from a very humble background, even by black standards, through discipline and self-study, Lembede managed to acquire a high level of education and took it upon himself to partake of the struggle for a free South Africa....
