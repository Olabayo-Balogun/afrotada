    Black Tax: Burden or Ubuntu

    by Niq Mhlongo

    This book is a collection of essays from different authors. It uses the personal experiences of these authors to explore the idea of black tax. The contributions from the different authors investigate the historical roots of black tax and the experiences of black families more specifically the black middle class.

    I enjoyed this anthology because many of the stories are relatable. The essays are well written and purpose of this book is to contribute to the dialogue regarding this social phenomenon. The discussion is centred around the impact of black tax.

    This anthology does get repetitive and I felt that most of the essays are one sided. They lean more towards black tax being “Ubuntu” and very little authors providing an opposing view.
